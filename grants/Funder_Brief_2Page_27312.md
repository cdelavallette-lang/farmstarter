# Funder Brief (2-Page)
## Regenerative One-Acre Farm Expansion | Pittsboro, NC 27312

## 1) Project Overview
This project develops a one-acre regenerative specialty-crop farm (currently ~0.5 acre active) into a profitable, climate-resilient operation using soil-first management, season extension, and practical automation.

### Current Conditions
- Location: Chatham County, NC (27312)
- Total acreage: 1 acre
- Active acreage: ~0.5 acre
- Key constraints: heavy clay/rocky soil, seasonal revenue gaps, wildlife/pest pressure

### Funding Purpose
Accelerate infrastructure and management upgrades that improve profitability, conservation outcomes, and year-round reliability.

---

## 2) Why This Project Matters
- Increases local supply of fresh specialty crops
- Builds a replicable model for small-acre farms in NC
- Prioritizes low-chemical, regenerative production
- Tracks measurable outcomes for transparent reporting

---

## 3) Use of Funds (Program-Dependent)
1. Season extension (high tunnel/greenhouse components)
2. Drip irrigation and water controls
3. Drainage/erosion mitigation
4. Soil-building systems (cover crops + compost)
5. Pest/wildlife exclusion systems
6. Monitoring tools (sensors/scouting workflow)

---

## 4) Expected Outcomes (24 Months)
- Expanded, more stable year-round production
- Improved soil and water management performance
- Reduced avoidable crop losses from pests/wildlife/weather
- Stronger farm economics and second-income durability

### Financial Projection (Conservative)
- Year 1 gross: ~$28,000
- Year 1 operating expenses: ~$20,700
- Year 1 net (before major capex): ~$7,300
- Year 2 gross: ~$48,500
- Year 2 operating expenses: ~$28,000
- Year 2 net (before major capex): ~$20,500

---

## 5) Measurement and Reporting
Monthly KPI tracking and quarterly summaries:
- Bed occupancy (%)
- Harvest volume (lbs)
- Gross sales ($)
- Buyer count/retention
- Water-use indicators
- Pest/wildlife incidents
- Soil trend indicators

Reporting cadence: Monthly internal updates + quarterly funder-facing summary.

---

## 6) Implementation Timeline
### Q1
- Baseline tests, mapping, procurement, technical coordination

### Q2
- Install and deploy infrastructure; optimize first production cycles

### Q3
- Peak-season execution; channel expansion; continuous KPI tracking

### Q4
- Year-end reporting, winter strategy, and next-cycle improvements
