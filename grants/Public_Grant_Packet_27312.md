# Public Grant Packet
## Regenerative One-Acre Farm Expansion Project
### Pittsboro, Chatham County, North Carolina (27312)

---

## 1) Executive Summary
This project transforms underused residential farm acreage into a profitable, regenerative specialty-crop operation designed for year-round resilience and measurable conservation outcomes. The site includes 1 acre total with approximately 0.5 acre currently active. Current constraints include heavy clay and rocky soils, seasonal production limitations, and wildlife/pest pressure.

Funding will support practical infrastructure and management upgrades including season extension, irrigation efficiency, soil-building systems, and low-chemical pest/wildlife management. A phased implementation model and KPI tracking framework are already in place.

Conservative financial projections estimate gross revenue growth from approximately $28,000 in Year 1 to approximately $48,500 in Year 2 as systems stabilize and market channels mature.

---

## 3) Problem and Opportunity
### Existing Constraints
1. Heavy clay/rocky soil limits infiltration and root development.
2. Limited protected production creates seasonal revenue gaps.
3. Wildlife and pest pressure causes avoidable crop loss.
4. Manual workflows increase labor intensity and reduce consistency.

### Opportunity
A one-acre intensive, regenerative model can produce high-value specialty crops with strong local demand while improving soil and water performance. With strategic infrastructure and management support, this project can become a durable second income stream and a replicable model for similar small-acre farms.

---

## 4) Project Goals
1. **Increase profitable production capacity** through season extension and improved field systems.
2. **Improve soil and water outcomes** with regenerative practices and efficient irrigation.
3. **Reduce production risk** through exclusion, monitoring, and operational upgrades.
4. **Build verifiable grant outcomes** using standardized KPI and recordkeeping systems.

---

## 5) Proposed Use of Funds
Funding is requested for the following categories (program-dependent):

- Season-extension infrastructure (high tunnel/greenhouse components)
- Drip irrigation and water-control systems
- Drainage/erosion mitigation where needed
- Soil-building inputs and cover-crop implementation
- Exclusion tools for pest/wildlife management
- Practical monitoring automation (sensors, scouting support)

---

## 6) Production and Management Model
### Enterprise Mix (Initial)
- 0.20 ac: salad greens + culinary herbs
- 0.10 ac: ginger/turmeric
- 0.10 ac: cut flowers
- 0.10 ac: perennial/pollinator support strips

### Regenerative Practices
- Permanent bed strategy
- Cover-crop rotation (warm + cool season)
- Compost-first soil improvement
- Reduced tillage and compaction control

### Risk Reduction
- Exclusion netting/row cover/fencing as needed
- Weekly scouting and incident logs
- Channel diversification (CSA + chefs + market)

---

## 7) Technology and Automation Plan
### Phase 1 (low-cost, immediate)
- Soil moisture sensors
- Smart irrigation timing/control
- Structured scouting and response logs

### Phase 2 (scaled)
- Drone-assisted scouting and perimeter checks
- Expanded climate-control integration for protected systems
- KPI-driven operational decision cycles
