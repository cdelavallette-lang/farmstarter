# Premium Ginger & Turmeric - Detailed Project Plan

**Project Owner:** silvia de la Vallette  
**Project Size:** 0.10 acre (4,350 sq ft)  
**Timeline:** March 2026 - November 2027  
**Revenue Target:** $8,000-$12,000/year

---

## Overview
High-value specialty crops requiring 7-month growing season and protected culture. Fresh ginger and turmeric sell for $8-$15/lb to specialty grocers, restaurants, and direct consumers. This project requires high tunnel installation for optimal yield and quality.

## Phase 1: Infrastructure & Grants (January - August 2026)

### January - February 2026
**Task:** High tunnel grant applications  
**Time Required:** 12-15 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1-2: Research NRCS EQIP high tunnel cost-share (50-75% reimbursement) - 3 hours
- Week 2-3: Get quotes from 3 high tunnel suppliers (30x48 recommended) - 4 hours
- Week 3-4: Work with NRCS conservation planner on application - 3 hours
- February: Complete NC AgVentures application including tunnel in budget - 3 hours

**Estimated Costs:**
- 30x48 high tunnel: $5,000-$8,000
- EQIP cost-share: $2,500-$6,000 (50-75% reimbursement)
- NC AgVentures: Up to $10,000 (can cover remaining costs with 15% match)

**Grant Deadlines:**
- NRCS EQIP: March 15, 2026
- NC AgVentures: April 30, 2026

### March 2026
**Task:** Submit applications and site prep  
**Time Required:** 8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- March 1: Submit EQIP application - 1 hour
- March 15: Mark out high tunnel location (flat, well-drained area) - 2 hours
- March 20: Soil test tunnel site - 1 hour
- March 25: Order ginger and turmeric rhizomes for May planting - 2 hours research/ordering

**Orders to Place:**
- Seed rhizomes: 50-75 lbs ginger, 25-40 lbs turmeric ($500-$800)
- Order from: Hawaiian Ginger Co., Tropilab, or local specialty growers
- **Timing Critical:** Order by late March for May delivery

### April - August 2026
**Task:** EQIP approval waiting period and backup planning  
**Time Required:** Variable  
**Action Items:**
- April: Expect EQIP decision (typically 4-8 weeks after deadline)
- April 30: Submit NC AgVentures application as backup/supplement - 2 hours
- May-August: If EQIP approved, wait for installation approval and order tunnel
- **Backup Plan:** If grants delayed, consider small-scale planting in raised beds with heavy mulch

**Decision Point:**
- If EQIP **approved** by June: Install tunnel in August-September for fall planting
- If EQIP **delayed** past June: Postpone ginger/turmeric to 2027, focus on other crops in 2026

**Time Required:** 24-32 hours (2-3 people, 2-3 days)  
**Action Items:**

- Week 4: Test ventilation, doors, and environmental controls - 2 hours

## Phase 3: Planting & Growing (May 2027 - November 2027)

### May 2027
**Task:** Planting ginger and turmeric  
**Time Required:** 12-16 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Pre-sprout rhizomes indoors in trays (plant in moist potting mix, keep at 75-80°F) - 3 hours setup
- Week 2-3: Monitor sprouting (10-14 days) - 30 min daily checks
- Week 3: Prepare tunnel beds with compost and create mounded rows - 6 hours
- Week 4: Transplant sprouted rhizomes 6-8" apart in rows - 6 hours

**Planting Details:**
- Ginger: 6-8" spacing, 2-3" deep, rows 12" apart
- Turmeric: 8-10" spacing, 2-3" deep, rows 12" apart
- Expected yield: 4-6 lbs per plant (ginger), 2-4 lbs per plant (turmeric)

**Orders to Place:**
- Seed rhizomes: $500-$800 (order in March for May delivery)
- Compost: $300-$500
- Mulch (straw or wood chips): $100-$200

### June - October 2027
**Task:** Growing season management  
**Time Required:** 4-6 hours/week  
**Action Items:**
- **Weekly (June-October):**
  - Irrigation: Check soil moisture 3x/week, water deeply (2-3 hours/week)
  - Weeding: Hand-weed around plants (1-2 hours/week)
  - Scouting: Check for pests (aphids, spider mites), monitor leaf health (30 min/week)
- **Monthly:**
  - Fertilize with compost tea or organic liquid fertilizer (1 hour/month)
  - Check tunnel ventilation, adjust as temperatures rise (30 min/month)

**Key Growing Tips:**
- Maintain 75-85°F during day, 65-75°F at night
- Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged
- Expect leaf emergence in 4-6 weeks, full canopy by August
- In September, reduce watering slightly to prepare for harvest

---

## Phase 4: Harvest & Curing (November - December 2027)

### November 2027
**Task:** Harvest ginger and turmeric  
**Time Required:** 16-24 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Stop watering, allow plants to begin dying back (signals maturity)
- Week 2-3: Dig plants carefully with fork or hands (avoid bruising rhizomes) - 8-12 hours
- Week 3: Wash rhizomes gently, remove excess soil - 4-6 hours
- Week 4: Begin curing process - 2-3 hours setup

**Expected Yield:**
- Ginger: 600-900 lbs (from 150-200 plants at 4-6 lbs each)
- Turmeric: 200-400 lbs (from 75-100 plants at 2-4 lbs each)

**Harvest Tips:**
- Harvest after first light frost (if growing in field) or when leaves yellow/die back (tunnel)
- Handle gently to avoid bruising
- Save best rhizomes for replanting next year

### December 2027
**Task:** Curing and sales  
**Time Required:** 8-12 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1-2: Cure rhizomes in warm (75-80°F), humid area for 7-10 days - passive time, 1 hour monitoring
- Week 2-3: Sort by size and quality (A-grade for fresh, B-grade for processing) - 4-6 hours
- Week 3-4: Package and deliver/sell to buyers - 4-6 hours

**Curing Process:**
- Spread rhizomes in single layer on screens or racks
- Keep in warm, humid space (garage with heater works)
- After curing, skin will toughen and flavor will concentrate

## Risk Management

### Common Challenges
1. **Grant Delays:** EQIP can take 6-12 months from application to installation approval
   - **Mitigation:** Apply early (March), have backup funding plan
2. **Temperature Management:** Ginger/turmeric need consistent warmth
   - **Mitigation:** High tunnel with optional supplemental heat source in winter
3. **Market Saturation:** Limited local demand
   - **Mitigation:** Pre-sell to buyers before harvest, develop online sales channel

---

## Key Success Factors

1. **Tunnel Installation Timing:** Must be installed by September 2026 for May 2027 planting
2. **Quality Seed Rhizomes:** Source from reputable suppliers, inspect for disease
3. **Consistent Moisture:** Drip irrigation critical for even moisture during growing season
4. **Market Development:** Start building buyer relationships 6 months before harvest
5. **Record Keeping:** Track yields, buyers, and pricing for continuous improvement
