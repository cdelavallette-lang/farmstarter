# Salad Greens & Fresh Herbs - Detailed Project Plan

**Project Owner:** silvia de la Vallette  
**Project Size:** 0.20 acre (8,700 sq ft)  
**Timeline:** March 2026 - December 2027  
**Revenue Target:** $12,000-$15,000/year

---

## Overview
High-demand, fast-growing crops for weekly sales to restaurants, farmers markets, and CSA customers. Focus on succession planting for continuous harvest March through November (field) and year-round under protected culture.

---

## Phase 1: Planning & Grants (January - March 2026)

### Week 1-2 (January 1-14, 2026)
**Task:** Grant research and application prep  
**Time Required:** 6-8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Review NRCS EQIP drip irrigation cost-share requirements (2 hours)
- Draft irrigation system design and get quotes from 3 suppliers (4 hours)
- Contact local NRCS office to schedule conservation planning visit (30 min)

**Grant Deadline:** EQIP applications typically due by March 15

### Week 3-4 (January 15-31, 2026)
**Task:** NC AgVentures application preparation  
**Time Required:** 8-10 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Complete NC AgVentures application form (4 hours)
- Gather required documentation: site plan, budget, business narrative (3 hours)
- Submit application (applications open February, due April 30)

**Orders to Place:**
- None yet - wait for grant approvals

### February 2026
**Task:** Conservation planning and site preparation  
**Time Required:** 12-16 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: NRCS conservation planner site visit (2 hours)
- Week 2: Finalize EQIP application with planner's recommendations (3 hours)
- Week 3-4: Soil test entire 0.20 acre area (2 hours field time + 1 hour sending samples)
- Week 4: Order compost (3-5 cubic yards) for spring incorporation (1 hour)

**Estimated Costs:**
- Soil testing: $50-$100
- Initial compost order: $150-$250

### March 1-15, 2026
**Task:** Submit EQIP application and begin early season prep  
**Time Required:** 8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- March 1: Submit EQIP application (1 hour)
- March 5: Mark out bed layout on 0.20 acre (3 hours)
- March 10: Spread compost if soil is workable (4 hours)

**Orders to Place:**
- Week 1: Order early season seeds if starting without irrigation (arugula, spinach, lettuce for direct seeding) - $50-$100

## Phase 2: Infrastructure Installation (April - May 2026)

### April 2026
**Task:** Install irrigation system  
**Time Required:** 16-24 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Submit NC AgVentures application (final deadline usually April 30) - 2 hours
- Week 2: If EQIP approved, order irrigation supplies; if not approved, purchase supplies directly - 2 hours ordering
- Week 3-4: Install mainline, drip tape, timers, and filters (12-16 hours with helper)
- Week 4: Test system and adjust for even coverage (2-3 hours)

**Estimated Costs:**
- Drip irrigation kit with timer: $800-$1,200
- EQIP cost-share (if approved): $400-$900 reimbursement
- Row cover/insect netting: $300-$500

**Orders to Place:**
- Irrigation supplies (early April)
- Transplants from local nursery: basil, cilantro, parsley starts (200-300 plants) - $100-$150
- Row cover and hoops for pest protection - $300-$500

### May 2026
**Task:** Major planting push  
**Time Required:** 20-24 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Plant first succession of lettuce, arugula, spinach (4 hours)
- Week 1: Transplant first round of basil and herbs (3 hours)
- Week 2: Install row covers over greens beds (2 hours)
- Week 3: Plant second succession of lettuce/greens (4 hours)
- Week 4: Weed and scout for pests (2 hours)
- Ongoing: Irrigate 2-3x per week as needed (1 hour/week)

**Orders to Place:**
- Mid-May: Order seeds for June succession planting - $50

## Phase 3: Harvest & Sales (June - November 2026)

### June 2026
**Task:** First harvest and begin weekly sales  
**Time Required:** 12-16 hours/week  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: First lettuce/greens harvest (4 hours)
- Week 2: Begin weekly harvest schedule (4 hours/harvest)
- Week 2: Plant summer succession (heat-tolerant lettuce, arugula) - 3 hours
- Week 3-4: Continue weekly harvests (4 hours each)
- Weekly: Weed, scout, irrigate (4-6 hours/week)

**Revenue Projection:** $800-$1,200 for June

**Buyer Outreach:**
- Contact 3-5 local restaurants with weekly availability list (use Buyer_Outreach_Templates.md)
- Set up farmers market booth or join established market
- Promote CSA shares or farm stand sales

### July - August 2026
**Task:** Peak production period  
**Time Required:** 16-20 hours/week  
**Action Items:**
- Continue bi-weekly succession planting (3-4 hours every 2 weeks)
- Weekly harvests (5-6 hours/week during peak)
- Weekly maintenance: weeding, scouting, irrigation (6-8 hours/week)
- Mid-August: Plant fall greens and herbs (4 hours)

**Revenue Projection:** $2,000-$2,500/month (July-August combined: $4,000-$5,000)

**Orders to Place:**
- Early July: Fall/winter seeds (spinach, Asian greens, arugula, lettuce) - $75-$100
- Mid-August: Fall herb transplants - $50-$75

### September - November 2026
**Task:** Fall production and season extension prep  
**Time Required:** 14-18 hours/week  
**Action Items:**
- September: Continue harvests, reduce succession frequency (4-5 hours/week)
- October: Harvest continues, prepare beds for winter cover crop (6 hours total)
- November: Final field harvests, clean up beds, seed cover crops (8 hours total)

**Revenue Projection:** $1,500-$2,000/month (Sept-Nov combined: $4,500-$6,000)

**Orders to Place:**
- September: Winter cover crop seed (rye, vetch) - $50-$75

## Key Success Factors

1. **Consistent Succession Planting:** Plant new crops every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest
2. **Pest Management:** Install row covers early to prevent flea beetle and aphid damage
3. **Irrigation Discipline:** Check soil moisture 3x/week, adjust timer seasonally
4. **Buyer Relationships:** Establish 3-5 reliable weekly customers by July 2026
5. **Record Keeping:** Use Farm_Forms_Pack.md templates for tracking plantings, harvests, and sales
