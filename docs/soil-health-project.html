Project Overview

Scope Whole-farm foundation
Timeline Ongoing (Year 1-5+)
Direct Revenue $400-$600/year CSP
Indirect Value $1,500-$2,000/year

Foundation project supporting all other crops. No product revenue but creates conditions for success of greens, ginger, and flowers projects. Generates annual stewardship payments through NRCS CSP program.

📊 Phase 1: Soil Testing & Assessment (January - March 2026)

January: Comprehensive Soil Baseline

⏱️ Time: 6-8 hours
  • Submit soil samples from entire 0.5-acre active area to NC Lab
  • Request: pH, macro/micronutrients, organic matter, texture analysis
  • Walk property and document problem areas (wet spots, compaction, erosion)
  • Map testing results and plan amendments
💰 Soil Testing Cost: $30-$60 per sample
Typical for 0.5 acre: 1-2 samples = $30-$60

February-March: NRCS Conservation Planning

⏱️ Time: 6-8 hours
  • Contact local NRCS office to schedule conservation planner visit
  • On-site consultation covering soil health, erosion, water management
  • Develop conservation plan together (FREE service)
  • Discuss EQIP eligibility and CSP enrollment
💰 Cost: FREE (NRCS service)
Value: $1,000+ in professional consultation

🥗 Phase 2: Soil Amendments (February - April 2026)

Lime Application (If Needed - January)

⏱️ Time: 4-6 hours
  • If soil pH < 6.0: Order agricultural lime
  • Typical NC application: 500-1,000 lbs per 0.5 acre
  • Spread lime on beds and water in to activate
💰 Lime Application: $50-$100
(Skip if pH already 6.0-6.5)

Compost & Organic Matter Application

⏱️ Time: 12-16 hours
  • Order high-quality compost (50-75 cubic yards for 0.5 acre)
  • Target: 2-3 inches deep on active growing area
  • Spread compost across beds (can do incrementally)
  • Incorporate into top 6-8" of soil in permanent beds
💰 Compost Cost: $300-$750 delivered
or $150-$300 if you pick up locally

🛏️ Phase 3: Permanent Bed System (April - May 2026)

Bed Layout & Design

⏱️ Time: 2-3 hours
  • Standard design: 4-foot beds, 2-foot walkways
  • For 0.5 acre: ~12 beds running 100+ feet
  • Mark out with string and stakes

Bed Construction Options

🆓 In-Ground
No frame needed. Permanent, $0 cost
💪 Wood Frame
4"×8'×100' beds. $200-$400 total
⭐ Metal Composite
Professional look. $600-$1,200 total

Permanent Walkways

⏱️ Time: 6-8 hours
  • Lay cardboard or landscape fabric on walkway areas
  • Cover with wood chips or straw to prevent mud and weeds
  • Benefits: Saves water, reduces weeds, no foot traffic on beds
💰 Walkway Materials: $100-$200

💧 Phase 4: Irrigation & Water Management (May - October 2026)

Main Water Infrastructure

⏱️ Time: 8-10 hours
  • Install main water line from source to field
  • Create central manifold with splitter valves
  • Simplifies management and reduces water waste
  • Coordinate with greens, ginger, and flower projects
💰 Main Infrastructure: $200-$400

Drip Line Installation

⏱️ Time: 8-10 hours
  • Run drip tape down each bed
  • Connect to main lines with flow control
  • Install timers if using electric pump (optional)
💰 Drip Lines & Timers: $400-$800
(Likely covered under EQIP irrigation cost-share)

Water Management Assessment

⏱️ Time: 4-6 hours
  • Document wet areas after rain (standing water > 4 hours)
  • Assess soil texture (clay, sandy, loam)
  • Plan improvements (raised beds, French drains, swales)

🌿 Phase 5: Cover Crop Rotation (Ongoing)

Spring 2026: Warm-Season Cover Crop

⏱️ Time: 4-6 hours per rotation
  • Allocate 30-40% of beds to cover crops
  • Warm-season mix: Buckwheat (4 weeks) + cowpeas + millet
  • Direct seed in vacant beds, let grow 8-10 weeks
  • Benefits: Fast nitrogen-building, erosion control
💰 Seed Cost: $50-$100 per rotation

Fall 2026: Cool-Season Cover Crop

⏱️ Time: 4-6 hours
  • Cool-season mix: Winter rye (rust) + hairy vetch + clover
  • Plant September-October, mow down March-April
  • Benefits: Winter soil protection, nitrogen fixation, weed suppression
  • Rotate beds annually (don't repeat same crop/location)
💰 Seed Cost: $50-$100 per rotation
💰 Estimated Savings: $200-$400/year on nitrogen fertilizer

🐝 Phase 6: Pollinator Habitat (May - October 2026)

Design & Site Prep

⏱️ Time: 8-10 hours
  • Identify 0.10 acre area for native plants (edge of field, perimeter)
  • Remove sod, loosen soil
  • Order NC native wildflower seed mix

Native Plant Recommendations

🌼 Black-Eyed Susan
Summer blooms, attracts bees
🦋 Milkweed
Monarch butterfly host plant
💜 Purple Coneflower
Late summer nectar source
🌸 Coreopsis
Long bloom season, low maintenance

Planting & Establishment

⏱️ Time: 6-8 hours + ongoing maintenance
  • Direct seed or transplant native plants in June
  • Water consistently for first month until established
  • Minimal maintenance: Remove invasive weeds in Year 1 only
  • Leave spent flowers/stems through winter for overwintering insects
💰 Pollinator Habitat: $100-$200 for seed/plants

🏛️ Phase 7: NRCS CSP Enrollment (August - October 2026)

Pre-Screening & Application Prep

⏱️ Time: 6-8 hours
  • Contact NRCS to request CSP pre-screening
  • List conservation practices you'll implement (beds, cover crops, native habitat)
  • Gather supporting documentation
  • Take baseline photos of current conditions

CSP Enrollment Benefits

💰 Annual Payment: $400-$600/year
📋 Duration: 5-year contract (renewable)
💵 Total Benefit: $2,000-$3,000 over 5 years
✅ Eligibility: You're automatically eligible with soil health practices

Eligible Practices

🛏️ Permanent Beds
Reduces erosion & compaction
🌾 Cover Crops
Soil health & nitrogen fixation
🐝 Native Habitat
Wildlife benefit & pollination
💧 Irrigation
Water conservation & efficiency

✨ Long-Term Soil Building Goals

Year 1 Targets: pH optimal, organic matter increase to 3-4%, permanent beds established

Year 2-3 Targets: Organic matter 4-5%, visible earthworms, soil structure improvement, water infiltration 1+ inch/hour

Year 5+ Vision: Organic matter 5-6%, thriving pollinator population, zero synthetic fertilizer needed, superior water-holding capacity, drought resilience
