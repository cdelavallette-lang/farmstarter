# Soil Health & Infrastructure - Detailed Project Plan

**Project Owner:** silvia de la Vallette  
**Project Size:** 0.10 acre (pollinator strips) + whole-farm infrastructure  
**Timeline:** Ongoing (Year 1 foundation, Year 2+ annual practices)  
**Revenue Impact:** Indirect benefits + $400-$600/year CSP payments

---

## Overview
Foundation project supporting all other crop production. Focuses on building healthy soil, supporting beneficial insects, improving water infiltration, and qualifying for NRCS stewardship program payments. This project generates no direct product revenue but creates conditions for all other projects to succeed.

---

## Phase 1: Soil Testing & Assessment (January - March 2026)

### January 2026
**Task:** Comprehensive soil baseline  
**Time Required:** 6-8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Submit soil samples from entire 0.5-acre active area to NC Cooperative Extension Lab - 2 hours
  - **Send to:** NCDA&CS Soil Testing Lab, Raleigh, NC
  - **Cost:** $6-$12/sample
  - **Include:** pH, macro/micronutrients, organic matter, texture analysis
- Week 2: Review last year's soil test (if available) to track changes - 1 hour
- Week 3: Walk the property and document problem areas (wet spots, compacted areas, erosion) - 2 hours
- Week 4: Map soil testing results and plan amendments - 2 hours

**Expected Results:**
- pH typically 5.8-6.5 in North Carolina clay soils (may need lime if below 6.0)
- Organic matter: Current likely 2-3%, target 4-5% for healthy gardens
- Macro-nutrients: Nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium analysis
- Micronutrients: Iron, manganese, boron, copper, zinc

### February - March 2026
**Task:** NRCS conservation planning  
**Time Required:** 6-8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Contact local NRCS office to schedule conservation planning visit - 30 min (January)
- Meet with NRCS planner on-site to discuss:
  - Soil conservation practices (permanent beds, cover crops, reduced tillage)
  - Erosion control needs
  - Water management and drainage
  - Wildlife habitat opportunities
- Develop conservation plan together - 3-4 hours (includes follow-up calls)
- Document existing practices and infrastructure - 2 hours

**Cost:** FREE (NRCS service)

## Phase 2: Immediate Soil Amendments (February - April 2026)

### February 2026
**Task:** Lime application (if needed)  
**Time Required:** 4-6 hours  
**Action Items:**
- **If soil pH < 6.0:**
  - Order agricultural lime based on soil test recommendations - 1 hour
  - Typical application: 1-2 tons per acre (0.5 acre = 500-1,000 lbs) - $50-$100
  - Spread lime on beds (hire spreader truck or apply by hand) - 3-5 hours
  - Water in to activate (same-day or next rain) - 30 min

- **If soil pH 6.0-6.5:**
  - Skip lime, proceed to organic matter building

### March - April 2026
**Task:** Compost and organic matter application  
**Time Required:** 12-16 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Order compost (high-quality composted bark or aged manure) - 1 hour
  - Target: 2-3 cubic yards per 1,000 sq ft of active growing area
  - For 0.5 acre (21,780 sq ft): ~50-75 cubic yards
  - Estimated cost: $300-$750 (delivered) or $150-$300 (pick up)
  - **Tip:** Order from local compost suppliers, landscapers, or leaf waste programs
  
- Week 2-3: Spread compost 2-3 inches deep on growing beds - 8-10 hours
  - Can do incrementally (1 section at a time)
  - Best done before main planting push
  
- Week 4: Incorporate into top 6-8" of soil in permanent beds - 4-6 hours
  - Use broadfork, rototiller, or hand digging

**Estimated Costs:**
- Lime: $50-$100 (if needed)
- Compost: $300-$750
- **Total Phase 2:** $350-$850

## Phase 3: Permanent Bed System (April - May 2026)

### April 2026
**Task:** Design and establish permanent beds  
**Time Required:** 20-28 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Design bed layout (4-5 feet wide beds, permanent walkways) - 2-3 hours
  - Standard: 4' beds, 2' walkways in 0.5 acre = ~12 beds running 100+ feet
  
- Week 2-3: Establish bed edges (raised or in-ground) - 12-16 hours
  - **Option 1 (Budget):** In-ground beds with no frame ($0, permanent)
  - **Option 2 (Mid-range):** Wood frame beds 4"×8'×100' ($200-$400 materials)
  - **Option 3 (Premium):** Metal composite beds ($600-$1,200)
  
- Week 4: Install permanent walkways - 6-8 hours
  - Lay cardboard or landscape fabric down walkway areas
  - Cover with mulch (wood chips, straw) to prevent mud and weeds
  - Cost: $100-$200 for materials

**Bed System Benefits:**
- No traffic on growing beds (improves soil structure)
- Easier to manage water (stays in beds, not paths)
- Defined area for soil testing and amendments
- Permanent reference points for crop rotation

### May 2026
**Task:** Install permanent irrigation infrastructure  
**Time Required:** 16-20 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1-2: Install main water line and manifold system - 8-10 hours
  - Coordinate with other projects (greens/herbs irrigation, ginger tunnel)
  - Single connection to main water supply with splitter valves
  - Simplifies management and reduces water waste
  
- Week 3-4: Install drip tape/soaker lines in each bed - 8-10 hours
  - Run drip tape down each bed, connect to main lines
  - Install timers if using electric pump

**Estimated Costs:**
- Main water infrastructure: $200-$400
- Drip lines for entire system: $400-$800
- Timers: $100-$200
- **Total:** $700-$1,400 (likely covered under EQIP irrigation cost-share)

## Phase 4: Cover Crop Rotation (Ongoing, Year 1-2)

### Spring 2026 (April - May)
**Task:** Warm-season cover crop planning  
**Time Required:** 4-6 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Assess which beds will be rotated to cover crops vs. production crops - 1 hour
- Week 2: Order warm-season cover crop seed mix - 1 hour
  - **Mix:** Buckwheat (4 weeks), cowpeas, millet - fast-growing summer cover
  - Cost: $50-$100 for 0.5 acre
- Week 3: Prepare bed (remove old crop residue, light tillage) - 3-4 hours
- Week 4: Direct seed cover crop in vacant beds - 2-3 hours

### September - October 2026 (Cool Season)
**Task:** Fall cover crop rotation  
**Time Required:** 6-8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Terminate warm-season cover crop (mow and leave, or till in) - 2 hours
- Week 2: Prepare bed for fall cover crop - 2 hours
- Week 3: Order and seed cool-season cover crop - 2 hours
  - **Mix:** Winter rye (rust), hairy vetch, clover - protects soil through winter
  - Cost: $50-$100 for 0.5 acre
- Week 4: Monitor germination - 1 hour

**Cover Crop Rotation Schedule:**
- Spring: Buckwheat + legumes (warm, fast, nitrogen-building)
- Fall: Rye + vetch (winter protection, nitrogen-building)
- Alternate beds: Keep 60% in production, 40% in cover crop rotation

**Benefits:**
- Nitrogen fixation (vetch, clover) reduces fertilizer needs ($200-$400/year savings)
- Erosion control during dormant seasons
- Organic matter building
- Weed suppression

## Phase 5: Pollinator & Beneficial Insect Habitat (May - October 2026)

### May 2026
**Task:** Design and install pollinator strips  
**Time Required:** 8-10 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Identify 0.10 acre area for native pollinator habitat - 1 hour
  - Ideal: Edge of field, around perimeter, or between crop blocks
  
- Week 2-3: Prepare site (remove sod, loosen soil) - 4-5 hours
  - Can remove grass sod and compost for other uses
  
- Week 4: Order native wildflower seed mix - 1 hour
  - **NC Native Mix:** Black-eyed Susan, coreopsis, milkweed (Asclepias), bee balm, purple coneflower, asters
  - Cost: $100-$200 for 0.10 acre
  - **Suppliers:** Prairiemoon.com, Seedsnow.com, local native plant nurseries

### June 2026
**Task:** Plant and establish pollinator strips  
**Time Required:** 6-8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1-2: Direct seed or transplant native plants - 4-6 hours
  - Spread seeds on loosened soil, rake lightly
  - Water consistently for first month until established
  
- June-August: Minimal maintenance, water during dry periods - 1-2 hours/month
  - Monitor for invasive weeds (remove by hand in first year)

### Fall 2026
**Task:** Pollinator strip winter prep  
**Time Required:** 2-4 hours  
**Action Items:**
- September-October: Leave spent flowers and stems for overwintering insects - 0 hours (passive)
- October: Clear invasive weeds - 2-4 hours
- November: Document pollinators and beneficial insects - 1 hour

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Attract native bees, butterflies, beneficial wasps
- Reduce pest pressure on crops through biological control
- Seed production for free plants next year

## Phase 6: Water Management & Drainage (May - October 2026)

### May 2026
**Task:** Assess drainage and water infiltration  
**Time Required:** 4-6 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Document wet areas after rain (standing water > 4 hours) - 1 hour
- Week 2: Assess soil texture (clay, sandy, loam) in different areas - 1 hour
- Week 3: Plan water management improvements - 2 hours
  - **For clay:** Build raised beds 6-8" above native soil
  - **For sandy:** Add compost to improve water retention
  - **For compacted:** Break compaction with broadfork or deep-dig

### June - October 2026
**Task:** Install water management features (if needed)  
**Time Required:** 8-16 hours (varies by site conditions)  
**Action Items:**
- **Raised bed system:** Already covered in Phase 3
- **French drain installation (if wet areas):** 8-10 hours labor
  - Dig trench 18-24" deep, add drainage pipe, backfill with gravel/compost
  - Cost: $200-$400 materials
  - **Likely EQIP-eligible:** Apply with conservation plan
- **Swale creation:** 4-6 hours
  - Dig shallow (6-12") swale to direct water away from problem areas
  - Plant with rain garden plants to capture/slow water

**Cost:** $0-$600 (depending on interventions needed)

## Phase 7: NRCS CSP Program Enrollment (August - October 2026)

### August 2026
**Task:** CSP pre-screening and application prep  
**Time Required:** 6-8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1-2: Contact NRCS to request CSP pre-screening - 30 min
- Week 2-3: Develop list of conservation practices you'll implement - 2 hours
  - **Eligible practices on your farm:**
    1. Permanent bed system (reduces erosion and compaction)
    2. Cover crop rotation (soil health, erosion control)
    3. Native plant pollinator habitat (wildlife benefit)
    4. Nutrient management (custom farming plan)
    5. Irrigation system (water conservation, if included)
    6. Residue/tillage management (soil structure protection)
- Week 3-4: Gather supporting documentation - 3-4 hours

### September - October 2026
**Task:** CSP enrollment  
**Time Required:** 4-6 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: CSP ranking period ends (usually September 30)
- Week 2-4: NRCS offers contracts to highest-ranked applications
- Upon offer: Review contract and sign (2-3 hours)

**CSP Benefits:**
- **Annual payment:** $400-$600/year for maintaining soil health practices
- **Duration:** 5-year contract (renewable)
- **Total benefit 5 years:** $2,000-$3,000 (additional baseline income)
- **Year 1 in program:** Often higher payment ($800-$1,000) to build practices

**Application Tips:**
- Emphasize innovative practices (permanent beds, diverse cover crops)
- Focus on soil health and water conservation benefits
- Document baseline conditions with photos and soil test

## Long-Term Soil Building Goals

### Year 1 Targets
- pH: Optimal 6.0-6.5 (adjust if needed)
- Organic matter: Increase to 3-4%
- Permanent bed system established
- Cover crop rotation started

### Year 2-3 Targets
- Organic matter: Reach 4-5% (rich, dark soil)
- Soil life: Visible earthworms, improved structure
- Water infiltration: 1+ inch per hour (from <0.5 initially)
- CSP contract: Active with consistent annual payments

### Year 5+ Vision
- Organic matter: 5-6% (excellent soil)
- Native pollinator population: Thriving
- Zero synthetic fertilizer inputs (cover crops supply N)
- Soil water-holding capacity: Reduced drought stress
- Farm reputation: Known for "living soil" practices

## Key Success Factors

1. **Soil Testing Annual Baseline:** Test every 2-3 years to track organic matter building
2. **Consistent Cover Cropping:** Rotate 30-40% of beds into cover crops annually
3. **Permanent Infrastructure:** Once established, beds and walkways are permanent assets
4. **Pollinator Habitat:** Plant once, maintain minimal - high wildlife benefit
5. **CSP Enrollment:** Free annual payments ($400-$600) for practices you're already doing
