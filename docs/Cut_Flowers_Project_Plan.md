# Cut Flowers - Detailed Project Plan

**Project Owner:** silvia de la Vallette  
**Project Size:** 0.10 acre (4,350 sq ft)  
**Timeline:** March 2026 - November 2026  
**Revenue Target:** $5,000-$8,000/year

---

## Overview
Fast-growing, high-demand flowers for farmers markets, farm stands, wedding/event sales, and CSA bouquets. Focus on easy, productive varieties that perform well in North Carolina climate with minimal inputs.

---

## Phase 1: Planning & Site Prep (January - March 2026)

### January 2026
**Task:** Variety selection and grant research  
**Time Required:** 6-8 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1-2: Research top-performing varieties for NC (zinnias, sunflowers, celosia, snapdragons, cosmos) - 3 hours
- Week 2-3: Research SARE Producer Grant for flower variety trials (up to $15,000) - 2 hours
- Week 4: Order seed catalogs or browse online (Johnny's Seeds, Floret, Harris Seeds) - 2 hours

**Recommended Varieties:**
- **Zinnias:** Benary's Giant Mix, Queen Lime series (high yield, long vase life)
- **Sunflowers:** ProCut series, Moulin Rouge (branching, multi-stem)
- **Celosia:** Chief series (plumed), Cramer's Burgundy (striking color)
- **Snapdragons:** Potomac series (heat-tolerant for NC)
- **Cosmos:** Double Click series (fully double blooms)

### February 2026
**Task:** Grant application and site layout  
**Time Required:** 8-10 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1-2: Draft SARE Producer Grant application (if conducting variety trial) - 6 hours
  - **Topic Example:** "Evaluating Heat-Tolerant Cut Flower Varieties for Coastal Plain Small Farms"
  - **Grant Deadline:** November-December for funding starting next year
- Week 3: Mark out 0.10 acre flower field (4,350 sq ft = ~60' × 70' block) - 2 hours
- Week 4: Soil test flower area - 1 hour

**Note:** SARE grants fund on-farm research. If not pursuing grant in Year 1, proceed with standard production.

### March 2026
**Task:** Seed ordering and soil prep  
**Time Required:** 8-10 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Order seeds for spring/summer planting - 2 hours
- Week 2: Spread compost (2-3 cubic yards) on flower beds - 3 hours
- Week 3: Install deer fencing around perimeter (critical for flower protection) - 4-5 hours

**Orders to Place:**
- Flower seeds: $150-$300 (buy bulk packs for succession planting)
- Deer fencing: 4' mesh, 200' length = $400-$700
- Drip tape: 200-300' = $200-$400
- Compost: 2-3 cubic yards = $100-$150

**Estimated Costs:** $850-$1,550

## Phase 2: Planting Season 1 (April - May 2026)

### April 2026
**Task:** Install irrigation and first plantings  
**Time Required:** 16-20 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Install drip tape down flower rows (6-8 rows, 60' long) - 6-8 hours
- Week 2: Direct seed zinnias, sunflowers, cosmos (cold-hardy varieties) - 4-5 hours
- Week 3: Start snapdragons indoors for transplanting in May - 2 hours setup, 30 min/week maintenance
- Week 4: Plant second succession of zinnias (2 weeks after first) - 2-3 hours

**Planting Schedule - April:**
- **Early April:** Sunflowers (ProCut Orange, Lemon), cosmos, first zinnia succession
- **Mid-April:** Celosia (direct seed or transplant)
- **Late April:** Second zinnia succession, more sunflowers

**Spacing:**
- Zinnias: 9-12" apart
- Sunflowers: 6-9" apart (for multi-stem production)
- Celosia: 9-12" apart
- Cosmos: 12" apart

### May 2026
**Task:** Succession planting and transplanting  
**Time Required:** 12-16 hours  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1: Transplant snapdragons started indoors (100-150 plants) - 4-5 hours
- Week 2: Direct seed third succession of zinnias and sunflowers - 3 hours
- Week 3: Weed beds and scout for pests (aphids, Japanese beetles) - 3-4 hours
- Week 4: Install support netting for snapdragons and tall flowers - 3-4 hours

**Orders to Place:**
- Support netting (Hortonova or similar): $50-$100

## Phase 3: Harvest & Sales (June - October 2026)

### June 2026
**Task:** First harvests and market setup  
**Time Required:** 10-14 hours/week  
**Action Items:**
- Week 1-2: First zinnia and sunflower harvests begin (6-8 weeks from planting) - 4-6 hours/week
- Week 2: Set up farmers market booth or farm stand sales - 2 hours setup
- Week 3-4: Continue harvests 2-3x per week - 4-6 hours/week
- Weekly: Weed, irrigate, scout for pests - 3-4 hours/week

**Revenue Projection June:** $400-$600

**Harvest Tips:**
- Cut flowers early morning or evening when cool
- Immediately place in water buckets
- Strip lower leaves before arranging
- Change water daily

### July - August 2026
**Task:** Peak production and continuous planting  
**Time Required:** 12-18 hours/week  
**Action Items:**
- **Weekly:** Harvest flowers 3-4x per week (morning harvest critical in summer heat) - 6-8 hours/week
- **Bi-Weekly:** Plant new successions of zinnias, sunflowers - 2-3 hours every 2 weeks
- **Weekly:** Deadhead spent blooms to encourage more flowers - 2-3 hours/week
- **Weekly:** Weed, irrigate, pest management - 3-4 hours/week

**Revenue Projection July-August:** $1,200-$1,800/month

**Peak Season Sales Channels:**
1. **Farmers Market:** $200-$400/market day (bouquets $10-$15 each)
2. **Wedding/Event Orders:** $300-$800/event (contact local wedding planners)
3. **CSA Bouquets:** $8-$12/bouquet weekly add-on
4. **Farm Stand:** $150-$300/week (self-serve honor system or staffed)

### September - October 2026
**Task:** Fall production and season wind-down  
**Time Required:** 10-14 hours/week  
**Action Items:**
- September: Continue harvests, zinnias and celosia peak again in cooler weather - 6-8 hours/week
- Late September: Plant cool-season flowers (snapdragons, sweet peas) for fall/winter - 3 hours
- October: Final harvests, pumpkin/fall decor sales synergy - 4-6 hours/week
- Late October: Clean up beds, compost plant material, seed cover crops - 6 hours total

**Revenue Projection Sept-Oct:** $1,000-$1,600

## Grant Opportunity: SARE Producer Grant

### Why Apply?
SARE funds on-farm research projects up to $15,000. Perfect for testing new varieties, growing methods, or market channels.

### Project Ideas:
1. **Variety Trial:** Test 8-10 heat-tolerant zinnia varieties for NC summer production
2. **Season Extension:** Compare tunnel-grown vs. field-grown snapdragons for winter sales
3. **Market Comparison:** Track profitability of farmers market vs. wholesale vs. event sales

### Application Timeline:
- **November-December 2026:** Application due (for 2027-2028 funding)
- **March 2027:** Notification of awards
- **April 2027:** Project begins

### Application Prep (10-15 hours):
- Define research question and methodology - 3 hours
- Create budget and timeline - 2 hours
- Describe outreach/education component (field day, blog posts, etc.) - 2 hours
- Write application narrative - 4-6 hours
- Get letters of support from partners - 2 hours

**Resource:** [SARE Producer Grant Application](https://www.sare.org/grants/)

## Key Success Factors

1. **Succession Planting:** Plant new flowers every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest
2. **Deer Protection:** Install fencing BEFORE planting (deer will devastate unprotected flowers)
3. **Early Morning Harvest:** Cut flowers before 9am for best vase life
4. **Market Diversity:** Don't rely on one sales channel; mix markets, events, and subscriptions
5. **Variety Testing:** Try 2-3 new varieties each year to keep offerings fresh
