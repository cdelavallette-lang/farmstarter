Project Overview

Space 0.10 acre (4,350 sq ft)
Season June - October 2026
Revenue Target $5,000 - $8,000/year
Key Crop 6-week to 12-week cycle

Fast-growing, high-demand flowers for farmers markets, farm stands, wedding/event sales, and CSA bouquet subscriptions. Easiest project to start quickly.

📋 Phase 1: Planning & Site Prep (January - March 2026)

January: Variety Research & Seed Selection

⏱️ Time: 6-8 hours
  • Research top-performing varieties for North Carolina
  • Review SARE Producer Grant for variety trials ($15,000 available)
  • Order seed catalogs or browse online suppliers

Recommended Varieties for NC

🔴 Zinnias
Benary's Giant Mix, Queen Lime (high yield, long vase life)
🌻 Sunflowers
ProCut series, Moulin Rouge (branching, multi-stem)
🌺 Celosia
Chief series (plumed), Cramer's Burgundy (striking)
💐 Snapdragons
Potomac series (heat-tolerant for NC)
🌸 Cosmos
Double Click series (fully double blooms)

February: Grant Application & Site Layout

⏱️ Time: 8-10 hours
  • Draft SARE Producer Grant application (if pursuing) - Topic: "Evaluating Heat-Tolerant Cut Flowers"
  • Mark out 0.10 acre field block (60' × 70')
  • Soil test flower area
🎯 SARE Grant Info: Applications due Nov-Dec (for next year)
Perfect for variety trials and on-farm research projects

March: Seed Ordering & Site Protection

⏱️ Time: 8-10 hours
  • Order seeds for spring/summer planting
  • Spread compost (2-3 cubic yards) on flower beds
  • Critical: Install deer fencing around perimeter
  • Order drip irrigation supplies
💰 March Costs:
Flower seeds: $150-$300
Deer fencing: $400-$700
Drip irrigation: $200-$400
Compost: $150-$250
Total: $900-$1,650

🔧 Phase 2: Infrastructure & Early Planting (April - May 2026)

April: Irrigation Installation & First Plantings

⏱️ Time: 16-20 hours
  • Install drip tape down flower rows (6-8 rows, 60' long)
  • Direct seed: zinnias, sunflowers, cosmos (cold-hardy varieties)
  • Start snapdragons indoors for May transplanting
  • Plant second succession of zinnias (2 weeks after first)
🔄 Succession Planting Schedule:
Early April: Sunflowers, cosmos, first zinnias
Mid-April: Celosia
Late April: Second zinnias + more sunflowers

May: Succession Planting & Transplanting

⏱️ Time: 12-16 hours
  • Transplant snapdragons from indoors (100-150 plants)
  • Direct seed third succession of zinnias & sunflowers
  • Weed beds and scout for pests
  • Install support netting for tall varieties
🎯 Key Milestone: First harvests expected June 2026

💐 Phase 3: Production & Sales (June - October 2026)

Month Main Tasks Hours/Week Revenue
June First harvests, set up markets, plant succession 10-14 $400-$600
July-Aug Peak production, 3-4x weekly harvest, deadheading 12-18 $1,200-$1,800/mo
Sept-Oct Fall production ramp-up, plant cool-season flowers 10-14 $1,000-$1,600

🛍️ Sales Channels (Multiple = Higher Revenue)

  • Farmers Market: $200-$400/market day (bouquets $10-$15)
  • Wedding/Event Orders: $300-$800/event (contact planners early)
  • CSA Bouquet Subscriptions: $8-$12/week add-on (10-20 customers)
  • Farm Stand: $150-$300/week self-serve or staffed

Harvest Best Practices

  • Cut flowers early morning (before 9am) when cool
  • Place immediately in water buckets
  • Strip lower leaves to prevent rot
  • Change water daily for longest vase life
  • Deadhead spent blooms to encourage more flowers

📊 Phase 4: Evaluation & Planning (November - February 2027)

November-December: Season Analysis

⏱️ Time: 6-8 hours
  • Calculate total revenue and costs
  • Track yields per variety (identify top performers)
  • Document customer preferences (colors, types, sizes)
  • Draft SARE grant application for 2027 (if pursuing)
📝 Key Questions:
Which varieties had highest yield?
Which colors sold best?
Which market was most profitable?

January-February: Year 2 Prep

⏱️ Time: 4-6 hours
  • Order seeds for 2027 (early = best selection & pricing)
  • Repair or replace fencing/irrigation if needed
  • Apply fresh compost to flower beds
  • Plan expanded varieties based on 2026 success

✨ Key Success Factors

  1. Succession Planting: Plant every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest June-October
  2. Deer Protection: Install fencing BEFORE planting (deer devastate unprotected flowers)
  3. Early Morning Harvests: Cut before 9am when flowers are hydrated
  4. Market Diversity: Don't rely on one channel; combine farmers market + events + subscriptions + farm stand
  5. Record Keeping: Track varieties, yields, customers, and sales to improve Year 2
